      Key Takeaways

      • June 2026 flipped the backlog trend: 48 fixed vs 42 new (net −6, fix rate 1.14). First backlog-shrinking month since February, with 0 new P0s and only 2 new P1s — both fixed within the month(s).
      • June re-classified: 88% are escape / implementation defects. 37 of 42 new bugs trace to a release (1 undefined); the rest are 3 legacy old bugs and 1 unfinished-feature gap. AI code-generation completeness alone accounts for 9 (undefined symbols, malformed elements, forbidden navigators).
      • Escape defects still concentrate in the Hikari/AI wave. Across 14 months (99 total): v542 (18), v549 (12), v545 (10), v551 (7) lead; June added fresh escapes from the v556–v560 feature train — Thunkable Cloud (3), AdMob/IAP (4), Asset Panel (3) and the Expo 56 upgrade (4, incl. the bricking P1).
      • Fix load stays concentrated. Mihail Duchev resolved 138 of 322 bugs (43%); Paulo Chaves and Ting Chou follow with 34 each — the bus-factor risk persists. Reporting stays QA-driven: Ioannis Roungeris (147) and Roberto Minhoto (115) filed 74% of 356 tickets.
      • Resolution discipline holds. June's resolved cohort hit 100% SLA adherence at ~8.5 business days average — the fourth consecutive month at 97–100%.
